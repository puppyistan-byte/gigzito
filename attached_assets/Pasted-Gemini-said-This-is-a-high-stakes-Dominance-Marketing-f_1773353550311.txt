Technical Summary: The GigJack "Seismic Event" & CRM Integration
1. The Audience Engine (The "My Audience" Checkbox)
Every Call-to-Action (CTA) on a user's post currently collects Name, Email, and Phone. This data needs to be "Liquid."

Data Aggregation: We create a user_audiences table that automatically maps every successful CTA conversion to the Creator's ID.

The Opt-In Logic: When a marketer hits "Go Live" or "Deploy GigJack," a checkbox labeled "Include My Audience" will trigger an automated broadcast to this CSV-derived list (Email/SMS).

2. The "GigJack" Seizure (The Frontend Takeover)
This is a State-Driven Interrupt. We use WebSockets (Socket.io) to push a "Seizure Command" to every active browser session on the site.

The "Freeze": A high-z-index overlay (z-index: 9999) locks the scroll and dims the background.

The Presentation: The "Hot Coupon" or Offer appears in the dead center with a "Seismic" CSS animation (shake/pulse).

The Collapse & Fly: Using Framer Motion or GSAP, the center modal "implodes" and animates its x,y coordinates to the top-right "GigJack Frame."

The Siren State: A persistent Red Siren SVG with a CSS animate-pulse and a CountdownTimer component stays active in the header until the offer expires.

3. The "Unfair Advantage" Approval Workflow
Because a GigJack is intrusive (it stops the user's "retina view"), it cannot be automated.

Admin Gatekeeper: The marketer submits the GigJack request. It enters a pending_gigjacks queue.

The "Seized" Status: Only once a Super Admin clicks "Approve & Launch" does the WebSocket signal fire across the entire platform.
