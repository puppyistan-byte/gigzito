The 1-Minute Cron "Safety Sync"
For Postgres, we won't do a full dump/restore every minute (that's actually very heavy on CPU/Disk). Instead, we use Postgres Logical Replication. It’s more "Big Time Company" style, but surprisingly easy on the CPU.

Why it’s better than a Dump/Restore:

Dump: High CPU (compressing data) + High Disk (writing a massive file).

Logical Replication: Low CPU. It only sends the changes (the "Delta") as they happen.

Step 1: The File Sync (The "Replace All" Nano)
Since you want your nano changes on hills to hit ash almost immediately, we'll put this in your crontab on hills.

Bash
# Every minute, sync all files from hills to ash
* * * * * rsync -avz --delete /var/www/html/ root@5.161.102.88:/var/www/html/
Result: You save the file, and within 0–59 seconds, it’s live on the other coast.

Step 2: The Postgres "Pulse"
On your current plan, we’ll set up Logical Replication. Here is the 1-minute logic:

Hillsboro (Publisher) keeps a log of every change.

Ashburn (Subscriber) checks in every few seconds and "pulls" only the new rows.

CPU Impact: Almost zero. It’s like a text message vs. sending a whole book.
