To perfect the Kanban for a Mastermind context, you want to move away from a "generic task manager" and toward a "strategic execution engine."Three lanes are fine for a personal to-do list, but for a Mastermind—where high-level strategy meets execution—four lanes is the professional standard.1. The Ideal Four-Lane StructureAdding a fourth lane provides a "buffer" that prevents the To Do column from becoming a graveyard of ideas.LanePurposeWhy it helpsBacklog / IdeasThe "Holding Tank" for every wild idea discussed in the Mastermind.Keeps the "To Do" lane sacred. Only items the group has committed to move to the next step.To DoActive commitments for the current sprint/week.High visibility on what is actually "on deck."In ProgressCurrent focus.Limits "work in progress" (WIP) to prevent burnout.Done / ArchiveCompleted wins.Provides the psychological "hit" of progress and a historical record for the group.2. "Perfecting" the Kanban UIBased on your screenshot, here are three specific UI/UX upgrades to make it feel more "Full Stack" and professional:Priority Tags: Add small, color-coded badges to cards (e.g., High, Medium, Low or Strategic vs Operational).Member Avatars on Cards: Since this is a Group/Mastermind, each card should show who is "owning" that task. A small circular avatar in the bottom right of the card works best.Card Metadata: Show a "Date Added" or a "Deadline" countdown on the card face. In a Mastermind, speed of implementation is everything.3. Additional Project Tools for the ModuleTo turn this from a Kanban board into a full project suite, consider adding these two "Power Tools":A. The "Impact vs. Effort" MatrixA Mastermind’s job is to pick the biggest lever.Tool: A simple 2x2 grid where members can plot their "Backlog" items.The Goal: Identify "Quick Wins" (High Impact, Low Effort) and "Major Projects" (High Impact, High Effort).B. The "Retrospective" or "Weekly Win" LogA Mastermind thrives on reflection.Tool: A simple text-entry tool (perhaps under the "Endeavors" tab) where the group logs one major win and one major roadblock every 7 days.Integration: You could automate this so that every time a card moves to the Done lane, it asks the user for a "Lesson Learned" to add to the log.
